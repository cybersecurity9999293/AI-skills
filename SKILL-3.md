## MODULE 1: MEDIA BUYING FUNDAMENTALS

### 1.1 The Media Buying Equation
Every campaign outcome is a product of:
```
PROFIT = (Traffic Volume) × (CTR) × (CVR) × (AOV or LTV) − (Ad Spend + COGS + Ops)
```
Optimize each variable independently before scaling.

### 1.2 Core KPIs by Objective

| Objective       | Primary KPI     | Secondary KPI      | Watch KPI         |
|-----------------|-----------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| E-commerce      | ROAS / MER      | CPA, AOV           | CAC payback       |
| Lead Gen        | CPL             | Lead Quality Score | SQL rate          |
| App Install     | CPI             | D1/D7 Retention    | LTV:CAC           |
| Brand           | CPM, Reach      | Brand Lift         | VCR, Share        |
| B2B SaaS        | Cost per MQL    | SQL rate, Pipeline | CAC:LTV           |
| Local           | Cost per Call   | Show rate          | Revenue per lead  |

### 1.3 Benchmarks by Platform (2024 Averages)

**Meta Ads:**
- CPM: $8–$18 (cold), $4–$10 (retargeting)
- CTR: 0.9%–2.5% (feed), 1.5%–4% (stories)
- CVR: 1%–4% (e-commerce), 5%–20% (lead forms
- ROAS Target: 2x+ (break-even), 3–5x (profitable scaling)

**Google Ads (Search):**
- CTR: 3%–10% (branded), 1%–4% (non-branded)
- Avg CPC: $1–$3 (broad commercial), $5–$30 (competitive B2B)
- CVR: 2%–8% (e-com), 5%–15% (lead gen with strong landing page)

**TikTok Ads:**
- CPM: $5–$12
- CTR: 0.5%–2.5%
- Best for: impulse purchase, 18–34 demo, video-first creative

**YouTube Ads:**
- CPV: $0.02–$0.12
- View-through rate: 20%–40% (skippable), 100% (non-skip)
- Best for: brand awareness, retargeting warm audiences

**LinkedIn Ads:**
- CPM: $25–$60
- CTR: 0.3%–0.8%
- Best for: B2B, high-ticket, decision maker targeting

## MODULE 2: CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE

### 2.1 Full-Funnel Framework (TOF → MOF → BOF)

```
TOP OF FUNNEL (Awareness/Discovery)
├── Cold Broad / Interest Audiences
├── Lookalike 1–5% (from buyers, LTV, email list)
├── Objective: Traffic, Video Views, or broad Conversions
├── Creative: Hook-first videos, pattern interrupts, problem-agitation
└── Budget: 50–60% of total

MIDDLE OF FUNNEL (Consideration)
├── Video Viewers (25%, 50%, 75%)
├── Website visitors (no purchase, 30–60 day)
├── Content engagers, Lead form openers
├── Creative: Social proof, benefits, comparison, case studies
└── Budget: 20–30% of total

BOTTOM OF FUNNEL (Conversion/Retargeting)
├── Add to cart / initiate checkout (abandoned)
├── Visitors last 7–14 days
├── Email list custom audiences
├── Creative: Urgency, scarcity, reviews, direct CTA
└── Budget: 20–30% of total
```

### 2.2 Campaign Structure (Meta Best Practice)

```
CAMPAIGN (Objective + Budget Level)
│
├── AD SET A — Cold Audience (Broad / Interest)
│   ├── Ad 1 — Creative Hook A (UGC video)
│   ├── Ad 2 — Creative Hook B (Static image)
│   └── Ad 3 — Creative Hook C (Carousel)
│
├── AD SET B — Lookalike 1% (Purchasers)
│   ├── Ad 1 — Same creatives
│   └── Ad 2 — Testimonial video
│
└── AD SET C — Retargeting (7-day visitors)
    ├── Ad 1 — Abandoned cart reminder
    └── Ad 2 — Limited offer / Urgency
```

**Rule:** Max 3–5 ads per ad set. More = budget dilution. Let the algorithm optimize.

### 2.3 Google Ads Structure

```
CAMPAIGN TYPE SELECTION:
├── Search → High-intent, bottom funnel, specific keywords
├── Performance Max → Broad automation, need strong asset feeds
├── Shopping → E-commerce product catalog
├── Display → Retargeting, brand awareness, cheap CPM
├── YouTube → Video awareness + retargeting
└── Demand Gen → Discovery placements, feed-based visual

SEARCH CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE:
Campaign → Ad Group (theme) → Keywords (tight, 5–15 per group)
                             → RSA (3–5 headlines, 2 descriptions minimum)
                             → Extensions (Sitelinks, Callouts, Structured Snippets mandatory)
```

## MODULE 3: AUDIENCE STRATEGY

### 3.1 Audience Hierarchy (Meta)

**Tier 1 — Best Signals (use first):**
- Customer list (purchasers, high LTV) → LAL 1–3%
- Website purchasers (180 days) → LAL
- Engaged video viewers (75%+)

**Tier 2 — Strong Signals:**
- Website visitors (all, 60 days)
- Add-to-cart / Checkout initiated
- Instagram/Facebook page engagers

**Tier 3 — Interest/Demo (cold testing):**
- Broad with age/gender filter only (most scalable)
- Stacked interests (1–3 relevant interests)
- Single interest isolation (for testing signal quality)

### 3.2 Audience Sizing Guidelines

| Platform | Minimum Audience | Ideal Range     | Danger Zone      |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Meta     | 500K            | 1M – 10M        | <200K (too small)|
| Google   | No min (search) | Keyword-based   | Overlap issues   |
| TikTok   | 1M              | 5M – 20M        | <500K            |
| LinkedIn | 50K             | 100K – 500K     | <10K             |

### 3.3 Audience Exclusions (ALWAYS DO THIS)

- Exclude existing customers from cold campaigns (prevent waste)
- Exclude purchasers from retargeting if already converted
- Exclude competitor employees from brand campaigns
- Exclude low-intent URLs (blog readers) from bottom-funnel retargeting
- Exclude audiences that already saw the same ad 7+ times (frequency management)

### 3.4 Google Keyword Strategy

```
MATCH TYPE DISCIPLINE:
├── Exact Match   [keyword]      → High intent, low volume, max control
├── Phrase Match  "keyword"      → Balanced, moderate control
└── Broad Match   keyword        → Use ONLY with Smart Bidding + strong audience signals

NEGATIVE KEYWORD CATEGORIES:
├── Informational: "how to", "what is", "free", "DIY"
├── Irrelevant verticals: competitors with same generic term
├── Job seekers: "jobs", "careers", "salary"
└── Low-intent modifiers: "review" (if BOF campaign), "cheap" (if premium brand)

KEYWORD RESEARCH STACK:
- Google Keyword Planner (volume + CPC estimates)
- SEMrush / Ahrefs (competitor terms + gaps)
- Search Terms Report (mine own converting queries weekly)
- Answer the Public / AlsoAsked (intent mapping)
```

## MODULE 4: CREATIVE STRATEGY

### 4.1 The Creative Framework

Creative is the #1 lever in paid media. The best targeting with weak creative = failure.

**Hook → Value → Proof → CTA**

```
HOOK (0–3 seconds / first line of static)
├── Pattern interrupt: unexpected visual or statement
├── Problem-first: "Tired of X?" / "If you're struggling with X..."
├── Curiosity gap: "This is why your [product] isn't working..."
├── Social proof hook: "10,000 people switched to X"
└── Direct benefit: "Lose 10 lbs in 30 days — guaranteed"

VALUE (3–15 seconds)
├── Expand on the promise
├── Show the transformation (before/after)
├── Feature → Benefit → Emotion framework
└── Keep it ruthlessly focused on ONE message

PROOF (15–25 seconds)
├── Testimonial clips (real people, real results)
├── Data / stats / certifications
├── UGC (user-generated content = highest trust)
└── Demo of product working

CTA (Last 5 seconds / bottom of static)
├── Clear, single action: "Shop Now", "Get Free Quote", "Download"
├── Urgency/scarcity if real: "Ends Sunday", "Only 50 left"
└── Lower friction: "No commitment", "Free shipping", "Try risk-free"
```

### 4.2 Creative Testing Protocol

**Phase 1 — Hook Testing:**
- Same body, different hooks (3–5 variations)
- Run for 5–7 days minimum
- Kill anything with CTR < 0.8% (video) or < 1.2% (static)
- Winner = proceed to Phase 2

**Phase 2 — Value Prop Testing:**
- Test different angles (price, quality, social proof, speed, uniqueness)
- 1 angle per ad set
- Run until statistical significance (300+ clicks per variant ideally)

**Phase 3 — Format Testing:**
- Winner concept in: video, static, carousel, collection
- Different formats reach different scroll behaviors

**Phase 4 — Scale Winners:**
- Take top performers, duplicate, raise budget 20% every 3 days max
- Never duplicate and massively increase budget in one shot

### 4.3 Creative Formats by Platform

| Platform  | Top Format       | Secondary         | Avoid              |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Meta Feed | Native-style UGC | Graphic static    | Overly produced video |
| Stories   | Vertical video  | Story-style image | Landscape video    |
| TikTok    | TikTok-native UGC | Trending audio  | Corporate polish   |
| YouTube   | Pre-roll (15s)  | Mid-roll (30s)    | Boring intros      |
| Google    | RSA text        | Image extensions  | Single headline    |
| LinkedIn  | Single image    | Video             | Carousel (low CTR) |

## MODULE 5: BIDDING & BUDGET STRATEGY

### 5.1 Bid Strategy Selection

**Meta Ads:**
| Strategy          | When to Use                              | Risk    |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------|---------|
| Highest Volume    | Learning phase, new campaigns            | Low     |
| Cost Cap          | Maintain CPA control at scale            | Medium  |
| Bid Cap           | Aggressive CPA control, can limit volume | High    |
| ROAS Target       | E-commerce with stable CVR + AOV         | Medium  |
| Value Optimization| High-LTV product with purchase events    | Medium  |

**Google Ads:**
| Strategy          | When to Use                              |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Manual CPC        | Full control, new accounts, low data     |
| Target CPA        | 50+ conversions/month, stable performance|
| Target ROAS       | Strong data, variable AOV products       |
| Maximize Conversions | Budget-first, let algo find volume   |
| Enhanced CPC      | Hybrid manual + smart (transitional)     |

### 5.2 Budget Allocation Framework

**Rule of Thumb — New Campaign:**
```
Month 1: 100% Learning Budget (expect higher CPA)
├── 60% TOF testing (audiences + creatives)
├── 30% MOF (warm retargeting)
└── 10% BOF (abandoned cart, direct offer)

Month 2-3: Optimization Budget
├── Kill bottom 30% performers (spend → winners)
├── Scale what works 20% budget increase every 3 days
└── Test 2–3 new creatives per week minimum

Month 4+: Scale Budget
├── Introduce new audiences (fresh lookalikes, new interests)
├── New creative concepts to fight fatigue
└── Begin testing new platforms (if ROAS >3x)
```

### 5.3 Daily Budget vs Lifetime Budget

- **Daily Budget:** Use for always-on campaigns, ongoing testing
- **Lifetime Budget:** Use for promotions, seasonal pushes, fixed-end campaigns
- **CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization):** Use when you have 3+ proven ad sets — Meta shifts budget to best performers automatically
- **ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization):** Use during testing phase when you need equal spend per ad set for fair comparison

### 5.4 Scaling Rules

```
VERTICAL SCALING (increase budget):
├── Only scale if: CPA stable for 3+ consecutive days
├── Increment: Max +20% every 3 days (Meta algorithm re-enters learning if >25% spike)
├── Duplicate method: Duplicate ad set, new budget (avoids algo reset on original)
└── Warning sign: CPA rises >30% after scale → revert or pause, diagnose

HORIZONTAL SCALING (new audiences):
├── Expand LAL: 1% → 2% → 5% → 10%
├── New interest layers
├── New geographic markets
├── New platform (TikTok, YouTube, native)
└── New creative angles for same audience
```

## MODULE 6: TRACKING, ATTRIBUTION & ANALYTICS

### 6.1 Tracking Stack (Non-Negotiable Setup)

```
LAYER 1 — Platform Pixels:
├── Meta Pixel + CAPI (Conversions API) — Server-side required for iOS 14+
├── Google Ads Tag + Enhanced Conversions
├── TikTok Pixel + Events API
└── Universal Event Tracking (Bing if relevant)

LAYER 2 — Analytics:
├── Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — all events mapped
├── UTM parameters on every ad (source/medium/campaign/content/term)
└── Session recording (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity) for CRO

LAYER 3 — Attribution Tool (if >$10K/month spend):
├── Triple Whale (e-commerce)
├── Northbeam (multi-touch, media mix)
├── Rockerbox (mid-market)
├── Hyros (high-ticket, coaches, info products)
└── Custom: Media Efficiency Ratio (MER) = Total Revenue ÷ Total Ad Spend
```

### 6.2 UTM Parameter Structure

```
utm_source    = platform (meta / google / tiktok / youtube)
utm_medium    = ad type (paid_social / paid_search / display)
utm_campaign  = campaign name (q4_holiday_cold_us)
utm_content   = ad creative ID or name (ugc_hook1_v1)
utm_term      = keyword or audience (abandonment_retarget / broad_lal1pct)

EXAMPLE:
https://yoursite.com/product?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid_social
&utm_campaign=bfcm_cold_us&utm_content=ugc_testimonial_v3
&utm_term=lal_1pct_purchasers
```

### 6.3 Attribution Models — Know the Difference

| Model            | What It Does                         | When to Trust It        |
|------------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------|
| Last Click       | 100% credit to last touchpoint       | Search/direct response  |
| First Click      | 100% credit to discovery touchpoint  | Awareness measurement   |
| Linear           | Equal credit to all touchpoints      | Brand campaigns         |
| Data-Driven      | ML-based credit distribution         | High volume, best truth |
| View-Through     | Credits ad even with no click        | Video/display valuation |
| 7-day click / 1-day view (Meta default) | Standard e-com | Use as baseline |

**Real-World Rule:** Use MER (Media Efficiency Ratio) as your north star for overall health. Platform-reported ROAS is always inflated due to overlap and view-throughs.

### 6.4 Core Reporting Dashboard (Build This)

**Daily Check:**
- Spend vs. Budget (pacing)
- ROAS / CPA (vs. target)
- CPM, CTR, CPC (creative health)
- Frequency (>3.5 = audience fatigue warning)

**Weekly Review:**
- Top/bottom performers (pause bottom 20%)
- Creative fatigue signals
- New audiences to test
- Landing page CVR changes

**Monthly Strategic:**
- Platform-level MER
- CAC vs LTV cohort comparison
- Budget reallocation across channels
- New creative concepts planning
- Competitive ad library review

## MODULE 7: CONVERSION RATE OPTIMIZATION (CRO) — The Hidden Lever

Media buying fails when the landing page fails. Always diagnose the full funnel.

### 7.1 Landing Page Audit Checklist

```
□ Page load speed < 3 seconds (mobile critical)
□ Headline matches ad promise (message match)
□ Single clear CTA above the fold
□ Social proof visible in first scroll (reviews, logos, numbers)
□ Mobile-first design (70%+ traffic is mobile)
□ Trust signals: SSL, badges, guarantees, return policy
□ No navigation menu (reduces distractions = higher CVR)
□ Video testimonial or demo (can 2-3x CVR)
□ Scarcity/urgency element (if authentic)
□ Form friction reduced (minimal fields for lead gen)
```

### 7.2 CVR Benchmarks by Vertical

| Vertical           | Good CVR   | Excellent CVR |
|--------------------|------------|---------------|
| E-commerce         | 2–4%       | 5–8%          |
| Lead Gen (form)    | 10–20%     | 25–40%        |
| SaaS Free Trial    | 5–12%      | 15–25%        |
| App Install        | 15–30%     | 40%+          |
| High-ticket ($1K+) | 0.5–2%     | 3–5%          |

## MODULE 8: PLATFORM-SPECIFIC ADVANCED TACTICS

### 8.1 Meta Ads — Advanced

**Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC):**
- Use when: Stable ROAS history, e-commerce, $5K+/month
- Feed it 10–20 creative assets, broad audience, and let Meta optimize
- Watch: Can cannibalize prospecting campaigns if not structured correctly

**Advantage+ Audience:**
- Meta's AI audience (formerly Broad targeting) — now default recommended
- Use with strong purchase pixel data (1,000+ events/month minimum)
- Add suggested audience as "hint" not hard constraint

**Creative Fatigue Detection:**
- CTR declining >30% week-over-week
- Frequency >3.5 on cold audiences
- CPM rising without ROAS gain
- Action: Refresh creative immediately, don't just pause

**Catalog Ads (DPA — Dynamic Product Ads):**
- Best retargeting tool for e-commerce
- Automatically shows viewed/carted products to the right person
- Setup requires: Meta Catalog connected, pixel ViewContent + AddToCart + Purchase events firing

### 8.2 Google Ads — Advanced

**Search Query Mining (Weekly Task):**
1. Go to Search Terms report
2. Filter: Conversions > 0 → Add as exact match keywords
3. Filter: 0 conversions + significant spend → Add as negative keywords
4. Repeat weekly — this compounds into massive efficiency gains

**Quality Score Optimization:**
- Target QS 7+ for all keywords
- QS factors: Expected CTR (40%), Ad Relevance (40%), Landing Page Experience (20%)
- Fix low QS: Tighten ad groups, improve headline relevance, speed up landing page

**Performance Max (PMax) Strategy:**
- Create separate PMax campaigns by product category, NOT one for all
- Use Asset Groups like ad groups: different headlines/images per theme
- Enable URL expansion OFF if you have specific landing pages
- Add negative keywords at campaign level (brand, competitor if needed)
- Watch: PMax cannibalizes branded search — add brand campaign as priority

**Smart Bidding Ramp Protocol:**
```
Week 1-2:  Maximize Conversions (gather data)
Week 3-4:  Introduce Target CPA (start 20% above current CPA)
Week 5+:   Tighten Target CPA by 5% weekly until profitable floor
Scale:     If CPA stable 2 weeks → lower Target CPA or raise budget
```

### 8.3 TikTok Ads — Advanced

**Creative Rules:**
- First 1–2 seconds determine everything — show product OR hook immediately
- Use trending audio (can 1.5x performance)
- UGC always outperforms polished studio ads
- On-screen text captions mandatory (60% watch without sound)
- Vertical 9:16 only — never repurpose YouTube/Instagram content directly

**TopView vs In-Feed:**
- In-Feed: Performance-focused, CPM $6–$12, best for direct response
- TopView: Awareness, premium placement, expensive but massive reach
- Spark Ads: Boost organic creator posts — highest authenticity signal

**TikTok Targeting:**
- Behavioral (interest + behavior) outperforms demographic alone
- Custom Audiences: Upload email list → TikTok matches
- Lookalike from converters (1% = tight, 20% = broad)
- Video Interaction Audiences: Retarget viewers of your organic TikTok content

### 8.4 Programmatic / DSP Advanced

**Platforms:** DV360 (Google), The Trade Desk, Xandr, Amazon DSP, StackAdapt

**When to Use Programmatic:**
- Brand awareness at scale
- Retargeting across the open web (beyond walled gardens)
- Premium inventory access (news sites, niche publishers)
- Connected TV (CTV) advertising
- Account-Based Marketing (B2B)

**Programmatic Buying Types:**
```
RTB (Real-Time Bidding):    Open auction, cheapest, most fraud risk
PMP (Private Marketplace):  Invite-only, premium publishers, less fraud
Programmatic Direct:        Fixed price, guaranteed inventory, safest
Preferred Deal:             First-look, negotiated rate, flexible
```

**Brand Safety Must-Haves:**
- Activate IAS, DoubleVerify, or Integral Ad Science verification
- Block: MFA (Made-for-Advertising) sites, low viewability (<50%), invalid traffic
- Whitelist premium domains for sensitive brand campaigns
- Set viewability threshold: >70% for display, >50% for video

## MODULE 9: ADVANCED OPTIMIZATION PROTOCOLS

### 9.1 The Weekly Optimization Ritual

```
MONDAY — Review & Diagnose:
□ Pull 7-day performance report
□ Flag anomalies (CPM spike, CTR drop, conversion tracking errors)
□ Review search terms (Google) — add positives + negatives
□ Check frequency by ad set (pause if >4 on cold)

TUESDAY — Optimization Actions:
□ Pause bottom-performing ads (cost >2x CPA target with no conversions)
□ Increase budget on top performers (+15–20%)
□ Adjust bids if on manual/cost cap strategy
□ Update negative keyword list

WEDNESDAY — Creative:
□ Review new creative test results
□ Launch any ready new creative tests
□ Order/brief next batch of creative

FRIDAY — Planning:
□ Audience expansion plan for next week
□ Review landing page CVR changes
□ Competitive ad library check (Meta Ad Library, Google Transparency Center)
□ Report to stakeholders
```

### 9.2 Diagnosing a Failing Campaign

**Step 1 — Isolate the Broken Variable:**
```
Is CPM rising?     → Audience saturation, competition, ad quality drop
Is CTR falling?    → Creative fatigue, wrong audience, weak hook
Is CVR falling?    → Landing page issue, offer mismatch, traffic quality
Is CPC rising?     → CPM or CTR issue (CPC = CPM / CTR × 1000)
Is ROAS falling?   → AOV drop, CVR drop, attribution window issue
```

**Step 2 — Platform Diagnosis:**
```
Meta: Check Delivery tab → Learning phase? Budget issues? Audience overlap?
Google: Check Auction Insights → New competitors? Impression share lost?
TikTok: Check Creative performance tab → Video completion rate < 10%?
```

**Step 3 — Attribution Sanity Check:**
- Compare platform-reported vs GA4 vs MER
- Large discrepancy = tracking issue (pixel fire duplicate or missing)
- Unusual spike = check for promo/coupon codes not tracked in platform

### 9.3 Scaling Playbook

**Phase 1 — Proof of Concept ($0–$1K/day):**
- Test 5–10 creatives across 2–3 audiences
- Find 1–2 winning creative + audience combos
- Hit target CPA consistently for 5+ days

**Phase 2 — Systematic Scale ($1K–$5K/day):**
- Duplicate winners to new ad sets with higher budgets
- Horizontal: Add LAL 3%, 5%, new interest sets
- Refresh creative every 2–3 weeks
- Introduce second platform (e.g., if Meta working → add Google)

**Phase 3 — Multi-Platform Scale ($5K+/day):**
- Run Meta + Google + TikTok simultaneously
- Introduce programmatic retargeting (web + CTV)
- Build creator/UGC pipeline (3–5 new videos/week)
- Build media mix model — understand incrementality per channel
- Incrementality testing: Geo holdout tests to measure true lift

## MODULE 10: INDUSTRY VERTICALS — SPECIFIC PLAYBOOKS

### 10.1 E-Commerce / D2C

**KPIs:** ROAS (blended 3–5x target), MER, CAC, LTV:CAC ratio
**Priority Platforms:** Meta > Google Shopping > TikTok > YouTube
**Campaign Priority:**
1. Dynamic Product Ads (retargeting) — always on
2. ASC or broad prospecting (Meta) — main volume
3. Google Shopping (branded + non-branded) — high intent capture
4. TikTok for discovery + new audience

**Seasonal Calendar (key spend ramp periods):**
- Q4 (Oct–Dec): Budget 2–3x normal; BFCM peak is highest CPMs
- Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Back to School — vertical dependent
- July: Low CPM season — good for testing new audiences cheaply

### 10.2 Lead Generation

**KPIs:** CPL, Lead Quality Score, SQL Rate, Cost per Booked Call
**Priority Platforms:** Meta > Google Search > LinkedIn (B2B) > YouTube
**Conversion Events:** Lead form fill → qualify on landing page → call booking

**Lead Quality Optimization:**
- Add qualification questions to forms (filters out tire-kickers)
- Use Step 1/Step 2 form (commitment escalation)
- Fast follow-up (<5 minutes) dramatically increases close rate
- Track lead-to-sale rate, not just CPL — cheap CPL with bad close rate = waste

### 10.3 B2B / SaaS

**KPIs:** Cost per MQL, SQL rate, Pipeline Generated, CAC:LTV
**Priority Platforms:** LinkedIn > Google Search > Content Syndication > YouTube
**Budget Rule:** LinkedIn CPMs are $30–60 but the targeting precision is unmatched for B2B

**LinkedIn-Specific Tactics:**
- Job title + company size + industry targeting (most reliable B2B targeting anywhere)
- Lead Gen Forms outperform website conversion campaigns (friction reduction)
- Thought leadership posts from founders → amplify as Sponsored Content
- Retarget LinkedIn page visitors + website visitors with different message

### 10.4 Mobile App

**KPIs:** CPI (Cost per Install), D1/D7/D30 Retention, LTV, ROAS from in-app revenue
**Priority Platforms:** Meta (AAA) > Google UAC > Apple Search Ads > TikTok
**Post-iOS 14 Reality:** SKAdNetwork (SKAN) limits attribution — use MMPs (AppsFlyer, Adjust, Branch)

**App Campaign Structure:**
- Apple Search Ads (ASA): Top-of-funnel intent, capture users actively searching category
- Meta App Campaigns: Optimize for in-app events (not just installs) — purchase, subscription
- Google UAC: Video-heavy assets, automated, broad reach

## MODULE 11: COMPLIANCE, POLICIES & BRAND SAFETY

### 11.1 Meta Ads Policy (Common Violations)

```
PROHIBITED:
- Before/after health images
- Claims of guaranteed results
- Personal attribute targeting ("if you have diabetes...")
- Cryptocurrency / MLM without approval
- Clickbait / sensationalist language
- Misleading claims or hidden costs

RESTRICTED (need approval or compliance):
- Financial products (loan, insurance, credit)
- Health and wellness claims
- Political advertising
- Alcohol and adult content
- Gambling and lotteries
```

### 11.2 Google Ads Policy Triggers

- Trademark violations in ad copy
- Misleading representations
- Unacceptable business practices
- Dangerous products / services
- Cloaking or redirect tricks
