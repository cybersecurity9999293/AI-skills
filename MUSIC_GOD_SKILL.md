### Electronic & Dance
- **House**: 120–130 BPM, 4/4, four-on-the-floor kick, offbeat hi-hats, Chicago roots (1980s), subgenres: deep house, tech house, progressive house, acid house, future house, afro house

- **Dubstep**: 138–142 BPM, half-time feel, heavy wobble bass, sub-bass focus, UK origins; brostep variant (Skrillex)
- **Trap (EDM)**: 70–80 BPM (half-time feel at 140+), 808 sub, rapid hi-hat rolls, festival drops
- **Ambient**: 60–90 BPM or no BPM, texture-driven, long reverb tails, Brian Eno defined the genre

### Scale Reference
- **Major (Ionian)**: W W H W W W H — bright, happy
- **Natural Minor (Aeolian)**: W H W W H W W — sad, dark
- **Harmonic Minor**: Like natural minor but raised 7th — exotic, dramatic
- **Melodic Minor**: Raised 6th and 7th ascending — jazz use, sophisticated
- **Dorian**: W H W W W H W — minor but with raised 6th, funky, smooth (Daft Punk love this)
- **Phrygian**: H W W W H W W — Spanish, flamenco, dark
- **Lydian**: W W W H W W H — dreamy, floating, John Williams loves it
- **Mixolydian**: W W H W W H W — bluesy major, rock staple (Sweet Home Alabama)
- **Locrian**: H W W H W W W — diminished feel, rarely used melodically
- **Pentatonic Major**: 1 2 3 5 6 — bright, country, pop
- **Pentatonic Minor**: 1 b3 4 5 b7 — blues, rock, metal, hip hop
- **Blues Scale**: Minor pentatonic + b5 tritone — maximum blues feel
- **Whole Tone**: All whole steps — impressionist, floating (Debussy)
- **Diminished (Half-Whole)**: H W H W H W H W — jazz tension, horror
- **Chromatic**: All 12 semitones — maximum chromaticism

### Rhythm & Time Signatures
- **4/4**: Universal, strongest downbeat on 1
- **3/4**: Waltz, 3 beats per bar
- **6/8**: Compound duple, two strong beats, each felt in 3 (ballads, trap)
- **5/4**: Irregular, Dave Brubeck "Take Five," Radiohead
- **7/8**: Balkan, odd-time prog, Tool
- **12/8**: Shuffle blues, gospel feel
- **2/2 (Cut time)**: Faster feel, classical/march
- **Polyrhythm**: Two different rhythms simultaneously (e.g., 3 against 4)
- **Polymeter**: Two different meters sharing the same beat (e.g., 4/4 vs 3/4 loop)

## 🔊 FREQUENCY SCIENCE — COMPLETE HZ GUIDE

### Frequency Bands in Audio
| Range | Hz | What Lives Here | EQ Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sub-bass | 20–60 Hz | 808 sub, kick sub, room rumble | Cut for small speakers; boost for big systems |
| Bass | 60–250 Hz | Kick body, bass guitar, low piano | Crucial for warmth; muddy if overloaded |
| Low-mid | 250–500 Hz | Vocal chest, guitar body, boxy zone | Often cut; "mud" zone |
| Mid | 500 Hz–2 kHz | Vocal core, snare crack, piano mid | Most important for presence and clarity |
| Upper-mid | 2–4 kHz | Vocal presence, guitar bite, harshness | 3kHz ear fatigue; 2kHz "nasally" |
| Presence | 4–8 kHz | Vocal air, clarity, attack transients | Boosts cut-through; excess = harsh |
| Air/Brilliance | 8–20 kHz | Cymbals, vocal shimmer, sparkle | Shelving boost for air; excess = sibilance |

### Specific Frequencies (Musical Math)
- **A4 = 440 Hz**: Universal concert pitch standard
- **A4 = 432 Hz**: Alternative tuning — claimed warmer (no scientific basis but popular debate)
- **C4 (Middle C)**: 261.63 Hz
- **Each octave doubles frequency**: A3 = 220 Hz, A5 = 880 Hz
- **Equal temperament**: Each semitone = previous × 2^(1/12) = 1.05946...
- **Just intonation**: Pure mathematical ratios (5:4 major third, 3:2 perfect fifth)
- **Pythagorean tuning**: Perfect fifths as basis (ratio 3:2)
- **Binaural beats**: Two slightly different frequencies in each ear create perceived third beat in brain
- **Solfège tuning (417, 528, 639, 741, 852, 963 Hz)**: "Solfeggio frequencies" — spiritual claims, not scientifically validated but widely referenced

### Psychoacoustics
- **Equal loudness contours (Fletcher-Munson)**: Human ear is most sensitive at 2–5 kHz; bass and treble need more volume to seem equal
- **Masking**: Louder frequencies mask nearby quieter frequencies (basis of MP3 compression)
- **Phantom center**: Identical stereo signal creates perceived center image
- **Haas effect**: Delay under 40ms creates direction perception; over 40ms heard as echo
- **Precedence effect**: First-arrival sound determines direction — use for mixing
- **Cocktail party effect**: Brain separates voices in a mix (importance of frequency separation in mixing)
- **Critical bandwidth**: Ear's frequency resolution unit — chords within one critical band sound fused

## 🎚️ PRODUCTION SCIENCE

### Signal Chain (Standard)
```
Sound Source → Microphone → Preamp → A/D Converter → DAW → EQ → Compression → Effects → Bus Processing → Master Chain → Output
```

### Gain Staging
- Keep input levels at **-18 to -12 dBFS** (RMS), giving **headroom** above 0dBFS
- Avoid clipping at any stage before intentional saturation
- Reference: VU 0 = approximately -18 dBFS in the digital world
- Plugin input/output should stay consistent (gain-neutral processing)

### EQ Principles
- **High-pass filter (HPF)**: Remove unnecessary low frequencies from non-bass elements (guitars, vocals, pads above 80–120 Hz)
- **Low-pass filter (LPF)**: Roll off harsh highs (drums above 15–18 kHz, bass above 150 Hz for sub focus)
- **Bell (Peak)**: Boost or cut a specific range — narrow Q for surgical cuts, wide Q for tonal shaping
- **Shelf**: Broad tonal adjustment — high shelf for "air," low shelf for "weight"
- **Cut before boost**: Subtractive EQ first — find problem frequencies, then boost what's missing
- **Phase consideration**: Linear phase EQ for mastering; minimum phase for mixing

### Compression Concepts
| Parameter | What It Does |
|---|---|
| Threshold | Level above which compression begins |
| Ratio | How much gain reduction (4:1 = every 4dB over threshold → 1dB output) |
| Attack | How fast compressor responds (slow = transient preserved) |
| Release | How fast compressor lets go (auto or tune to tempo) |
| Knee | Hard knee = abrupt; soft knee = gradual compression onset |
| Make-up gain | Compensate for reduced output level |

- **Slow attack (30–100ms)**: Preserves punch/transients — use on drums, bass
- **Fast attack (0.1–10ms)**: Catches transients — use for taming peaks
- **Parallel compression (NY compression)**: Blend compressed + uncompressed signal for punch with dynamics

### Reverb Types
- **Room**: Small, natural, subtle — close-sounding spaces
- **Hall**: Large, long decay — orchestral, dramatic
- **Plate**: Metallic, smooth — classic on vocals and snares
- **Spring**: Lo-fi, wobbly — guitar amps, vintage
- **Chamber**: Medium, smooth — classic recording studios
- **Convolution (IR)**: Real acoustic space captured — ultra-realistic
- **Algorithmic**: Synthetic — more adjustable, creative potential

### Key Mixing Rules
1. **Low-end management**: Only one element dominates sub (kick OR bass, not both)
2. **Mid-side processing**: Separate mono (mid) and stereo (side) information for surgical control
3. **Frequency masking**: If two elements share the same range, one must be cut, panned, or automated
4. **Loudness war**: Target **-14 LUFS** for Spotify, **-16 LUFS** for Apple Music (integrated loudness)
5. **Stereo field**: Keep sub bass mono (below 80–100 Hz); widen highs for space
6. **Reference tracks**: A/B constantly against commercial references at matched loudness
7. **Ear fatigue**: Take breaks every 45–60 minutes; mix at low volumes; trust your ears after rest

## 🎸 SOUND DESIGN — SYNTHESIS BIBLE

### Synthesis Types
- **Subtractive**: Start with rich oscillator, filter harmonics out — most common (Moog, Minimoog)
- **Additive**: Build sound from sine wave partials — Yamaha DX7 style
- **FM (Frequency Modulation)**: One oscillator modulates another — metallic, complex, DX7
- **Wavetable**: Scan through waveforms — Serum, Massive — modern standard
- **Granular**: Chop audio into tiny grains, rearrange — textures, pads, atmospheres
- **Physical Modeling**: Simulate physical instruments mathematically — Yamaha VL70-m
- **Sample-based**: Recorded samples triggered — most realistic
- **Spectral**: Operate in frequency domain — Iris, Falcon

### Oscillator Waveforms
| Waveform | Sound Character | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Sine | Pure, smooth, no harmonics | Sub bass, FM carrier |
| Triangle | Slightly hollow, soft | Flute-like, sub with body |
| Sawtooth | Bright, full harmonics | Leads, brass, pads |
| Square | Hollow, woody, strong odd harmonics | Bass, retro leads |
| Pulse | Sawtooth variants, thinner or fatter by width | Rich pads, detuned sounds |
| Noise | All frequencies, texture | Hi-hats, snares, breath |

### Filter Types
- **Low-pass (LPF)**: Cuts highs, warms sound — most used
- **High-pass (HPF)**: Cuts lows, thins sound
- **Band-pass (BPF)**: Passes middle range — wah, formant
- **Notch**: Cuts specific frequency — phaser base
- **Ladder filter**: Moog-style, warm 4-pole — bass/lead
- **Comb filter**: Resonant peaks — flanger base

### Envelope (ADSR)
- **Attack**: How fast sound rises from zero to full — short = punchy; long = swell
- **Decay**: Fall from peak to sustain level
- **Sustain**: Level held while key pressed (not time — it's a level)
- **Release**: How fast sound fades after key release

### Modulation
- **LFO**: Low frequency oscillator — creates periodic modulation (vibrato, tremolo, wobble)
- **Envelope → Pitch**: Pitch drop on kick, snare snap
- **Envelope → Filter**: Classic analog filter sweep
- **Velocity → Amplitude/Filter**: Dynamics from playing

## 🎤 SONGWRITING & LYRICS SCIENCE

### Song Structure Templates
**Standard Pop**:
```
Intro (4–8 bars) → Verse 1 (8–16 bars) → Pre-Chorus (4–8 bars) → Chorus (8–16 bars) →
Verse 2 → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Bridge (8 bars) → Final Chorus (often with variation) → Outro
```

**Hip Hop**:
```
Intro (optional) → Hook (8 bars) → Verse 1 (16 bars) → Hook → Verse 2 (16 bars) → Hook →
[Optional Bridge/Outro Verse] → Hook → Outro
```

**EDM**:
```
Intro (16 bars) → Buildup/Break (8–16 bars) → Drop (16–32 bars) →
Breakdown (16 bars) → Buildup → Second Drop → Outro
```

**Blues (12-Bar)**:
```
I I I I / IV IV I I / V IV I V (repeat)
```

### Lyric Writing Principles
- **Hook**: Most memorable part — must contain the title/central idea, repeat often
- **Verse**: Storytelling, context, detail — each verse advances the narrative
- **Pre-chorus**: Builds tension toward chorus — emotional lift
- **Bridge**: Contrast, new perspective, musical/lyrical break from repetition
- **Prosody**: The match between lyric stress and musical accent — crucial for natural flow
- **Internal rhyme**: Rhymes within a bar, not just at end (Eminem, Kendrick technique)
- **Multisyllabic rhyme**: Multiple syllables rhyming simultaneously (AABA at syllable level)
- **Assonance**: Vowel sound matching (lo-fi/lonely, hate/say)
- **Alliteration**: Consonant repetition at start (specifically speaking)
- **Show don't tell**: "I missed the funeral" hits harder than "I was sad when they died"
- **Specificity**: Concrete details over abstractions — "blue Dodge Charger" over "a car"

## 🎓 MUSIC SCIENCE — DEEP PHYSICS

### Sound Wave Physics
- Sound travels at **343 m/s** at 20°C in air
- **Wavelength = Speed / Frequency** (λ = v/f)
- 100 Hz wave = 3.43 meters long → needs a large room to reproduce fully
- **Decibel (dB)**: Logarithmic measure of sound pressure; every 6dB = double amplitude; every 10dB = perceived double loudness
- **SPL (Sound Pressure Level)**: 0 dB = threshold of hearing; 120 dB = threshold of pain; concerts: 100–110 dB
- **Fourier Transform**: Any complex waveform can be decomposed into a sum of sine waves — the mathematical basis of all audio analysis
- **FFT (Fast Fourier Transform)**: Computational version — what your DAW spectrum analyzer uses
- **THD (Total Harmonic Distortion)**: Measure of added harmonics not in original signal — tubes add even-order (pleasing), solid state adds odd-order (harsh)

### Acoustics
- **Standing waves (room modes)**: At low frequencies, waves reflect and create nodes (dead spots) and antinodes (loud spots) — major problem in untreated rooms
- **Room modes formula**: f = (c/2) × √((l/Lx)² + (m/Ly)² + (n/Lz)²)
- **Schroeder frequency**: Below this, room is mode-dominated; above, diffuse/statistical
- **RT60**: Time for sound to decay 60dB — ideal mixing room: 0.2–0.4 seconds
- **SBIR (Speaker Boundary Interference Response)**: Comb filtering from wall reflections near speakers
- **Acoustic treatment types**:
  - Absorption (rockwool, foam): Removes energy, controls reverb
  - Diffusion (QRD panels): Scatters reflections without removing energy
  - Bass traps: Corner-placed absorption for low-frequency control

### Music Math & Harmony Science
- **Circle of Fifths**: Each key is a perfect fifth (7 semitones) from the next; clockwise = sharps, counterclockwise = flats
- **Overtone series**: Physical series of harmonics above fundamental — 1f, 2f, 3f, 4f… natural intervals emerge from this (octave, fifth, fourth, major third)
- **Consonance vs Dissonance**: Ratios with smaller integers are more consonant (3:2 perfect fifth > 45:32 tritone)
- **Harmonic entropy**: Measure of pitch relationship complexity — lower entropy = more consonant
- **Shepard tone**: Auditory illusion of endlessly rising/falling pitch
- **Tritone substitution**: Substitute a chord with another whose root is a tritone away — shares two common tones

## 🏢 MUSIC BUSINESS

### Revenue Streams
1. **Streaming royalties**: Performance royalties (SoundExchange, PROs) + mechanical royalties (Harry Fox, DistroKid collects)
2. **Sync licensing**: Music placed in film/TV/ads/games — often highest single paydays
3. **Publishing**: Writer's share + publisher's share of compositions (PRO registration essential — ASCAP, BMI, SESAC)
4. **Live performance**: Touring, shows, set fees
5. **Merchandise**: Physical goods alongside brand
6. **Beat selling**: Lease ($30–$100) vs exclusive ($300–$2000+) vs trackout stems
7. **Sample clearance**: Licensing samples from original owners — master + sync license needed
8. **NFT music**: Blockchain-based music ownership and royalties
9. **Teaching/production courses**: Monetizing knowledge

### Distribution
- **DistroKid**: Unlimited releases, ~$19.99/year, fast, keeps 100% royalties
- **TuneCore**: Per-release pricing, keeps 100% royalties
- **CD Baby**: One-time fee per release, takes 9% mechanical royalties
- **AWAL**: Selective, takes % but offers support and advances for bigger artists
- **Amuse**: Free tier available, label services for signed artists
- **Stem**: Revenue split tool for collaborators

### PROs (Performing Rights Organizations)
- **ASCAP** (USA): Writer + publisher membership
- **BMI** (USA): Free membership for writers, lower threshold
- **SESAC** (USA): Invite-only, often higher payouts
- **PRS** (UK)
- **SOCAN** (Canada)
- **GEMA** (Germany)
- **Register every release with your PRO immediately after release**

### Copyright Basics
- **Master rights**: Who owns the recording (usually label or artist)
- **Publishing rights**: Who owns the composition (writer + publisher)
- **Work for hire**: Creator gives up ownership — avoid unless compensated fairly
- **Copyright duration**: Life of creator + 70 years (USA/EU)
- **Poor Man's Copyright**: Mailing yourself a copy has no legal standing — register at copyright.gov
- **Sample clearance**: Get permission BEFORE release — both master + sync clearances required
